E-commerce Personalization Research

Timeline

6 months

Team Size

8 people

Impact

34% increase in conversion

Industry

E-commerce

Challenge

A leading e-commerce platform was experiencing declining user engagement and conversion rates. The company needed to understand how personalization could improve the shopping experience and drive business results.

Research Approach

I led a comprehensive mixed-methods research initiative that included:

  • 60 in-depth user interviews across different customer segments
  • Large-scale survey research with 2,500 respondents
  • Behavioral analytics analysis of 6 months of user data
  • A/B testing of personalization features
  • Competitive analysis of personalization strategies

Key Findings

Our research revealed that users were overwhelmed by choice and wanted more relevant product recommendations. We identified three key personalization opportunities: browsing history-based recommendations, location-based suggestions, and preference-based filtering.

Impact

The research-driven personalization features resulted in a 34% increase in conversion rates, 28% improvement in user engagement, and 15% reduction in bounce rates. The insights also informed the product roadmap for the next 18 months.

Mobile App Accessibility Research

Timeline

4 months

Team Size

5 people

Impact

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance

Industry

Healthcare

Challenge

A healthcare mobile app needed to ensure accessibility compliance and improve usability for users with disabilities. The app served a diverse user base with varying accessibility needs.

Research Approach

I designed and executed a comprehensive accessibility research program:

  • Accessibility audit using automated and manual testing tools
  • Usability testing with 25 participants with diverse disabilities
  • Expert review sessions with accessibility consultants
  • Heuristic evaluation based on WCAG guidelines
  • Screen reader testing across different devices

Key Findings

We identified 47 accessibility barriers across navigation, content structure, and interactive elements. Users with visual impairments struggled with poor color contrast, while users with motor disabilities had difficulty with small touch targets.

Impact

The research led to achieving WCAG 2.1 AA compliance, improved user satisfaction scores by 40% among users with disabilities, and expanded the app's addressable market by 15%.

SaaS Onboarding Optimization

Timeline

3 months

Team Size

6 people

Impact

45% faster time-to-value

Industry

SaaS

Challenge

A B2B SaaS platform was experiencing high churn rates during the onboarding process. New users were struggling to understand the product's value and complete their initial setup.

Research Approach

I conducted a multi-phase research project to understand onboarding pain points:

  • User journey mapping through observational studies
  • Think-aloud usability testing with 30 new users
  • Analytics analysis of drop-off points
  • Post-onboarding interviews with successful and unsuccessful users
  • Competitive analysis of SaaS onboarding flows

Key Findings

Users were overwhelmed by too many options and unclear value propositions. The research revealed that users needed clearer progress indicators, contextual help, and a more guided experience to reach their first success moment.

Impact

The optimized onboarding flow reduced time-to-value by 45%, increased trial-to-paid conversion by 28%, and improved user activation rates by 35%. Customer satisfaction scores during onboarding improved from 3.2 to 4.6 out of 5.

Voice Interface Research for Smart Home

Timeline

5 months

Team Size

10 people

Impact

60% improvement in task completion

Industry

IoT/Smart Home

Challenge

A smart home company was developing a voice interface for their ecosystem of connected devices. They needed to understand how users naturally interact with voice technology and design intuitive voice commands.

Research Approach

I designed a comprehensive voice UX research program:

  • Ethnographic home visits with 20 families
  • Voice interaction prototyping and testing
  • Natural language processing analysis
  • Context-aware usage scenarios testing
  • Cross-cultural voice interaction studies

Key Findings

Users preferred conversational, natural language commands over rigid syntax. The research revealed cultural differences in voice interaction patterns and the importance of contextual awareness for smart home automation.

Impact

The voice interface achieved 60% improvement in task completion rates, 45% reduction in user errors, and 90% user satisfaction scores. The research insights informed the development of 15 new voice-enabled features.
